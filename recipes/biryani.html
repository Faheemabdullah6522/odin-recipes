Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful South Asian rice dish made by layering marinated meat and partially cooked rice, then cooking them together to perfection. To prepare biryani, start by marinating pieces of chicken, mutton, or beef in yogurt
mixed with ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Let it rest for at least an hour to allow the flavors to absorb. Meanwhile, soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then boil it with whole spices like bay leaves, cloves, cardamoms, and cinnamon until it is about 70% cooked.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala1
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • Water (for soaking and boiling)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 3-4 green cardamoms
  • -2 black cardamoms
  • 1 small cinnamon stick

For the Biryani Masala Base:

  • 2 large onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • A handful of fresh mint leaves
  • A handful of fresh coriander (cilantro) leaves

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, spices, ginger-garlic paste, and lemon juice; let it rest for at least 1 hour.
  2. Soak basmati rice, then boil it with whole spices until it's 70% cooked; drain and set aside.
  3. In a pot, fry onions until golden, add tomatoes, chilies, mint, and the marinated chicken; cook until the chicken is tender.
  4. Layer cooked chicken and rice in the pot, adding fried onions, coriander, mint, and saffron milk between layers.
  5. Cover tightly and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-30 minutes, then gently mix and serve hot.
